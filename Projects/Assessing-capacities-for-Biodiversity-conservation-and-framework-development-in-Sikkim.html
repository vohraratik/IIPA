Project Overview

The Global Environment Facility (GEF) funded project "SECURE Himalaya" is being implemented by the Ministry of Environment, Forests and Climate Change (MoEF&CC), Government of India in collaboration with the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in the states of Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and Sikkim. The project aims to promote sustainable land and forest management in high range Indian Himalayan ecosystems through a landscape-based approach. Under the SECURE Himalayan project, the Indian Institute of Public Administration (IIPA), New Delhi executed a project titled "Assessment of capacities and training needs of key stakeholders for long-term effective biodiversity conservation" in Sikkim, sponsored by UNDP. The goal of this project was to develop a capacity building framework to enhance the capacities of key stakeholders such as government line departments, research and training institutions, community institutions, and NGOs in Sikkim for long-term biodiversity conservation. The present study conducted a training needs assessment involving gap analysis to develop a strategy to train different stakeholders and bridge gaps at various levels. The report presents the methodology used, findings based on key stakeholder responses and expert consultations, as well as recommendations and comments summarized from consultations and expert opinions. Three training modules were developed as project deliverables to enhance the capacities of key stakeholders in Sikkim, including a generic module providing basic information on biodiversity, conservation, and mainstreaming, and two sector-specific modules focusing on Agriculture and Biodiversity, and Sustainable Tourism, respectively. The outlines of the three modules are detailed in the report.


Duration

December 2018 to February 2020

Funding Agency

Objectives

  • To assess the capacities and training needs of key stakeholders* for long -term biodiversity conservation, livelihood enhancement and Natural Resource Management (NRM)
  • To develop a framework and strategies for capacity development of the target stakeholders
  • To validate baseline data and refinement of capacity building indicators
  • To develop easy to understand modules and curriculum on identified areas/sectors
  • To conduct pilot training (Training for Trainers) programme in the landscape

Methodology and Analysis

This project was designed to ensure a comprehensive and effective assessment of the training needs of key stakeholders. The methodology involves the following steps:

  • Desk Review: A comprehensive review of literature, reports, policies, and best practices related to biodiversity conservation, capacity building, and sustainable development in the Himalayan region and Sikkim was conducted. That helped to identify gaps and challenges in the existing capacity-building initiatives and provide a foundation for the training needs assessment.
  • Stakeholder Identification: The key stakeholders involved in biodiversity conservation in the Sikkim region was identified. That includes government line departments, research and training institutions, community institutions, and NGOs.
  • Training Needs Assessment: A survey questionnaire was developed and administered to the identified stakeholders to assess their level of knowledge and skills related to biodiversity conservation and capacity-building initiatives. The survey also seek feedback on the capacity-building initiatives and identify areas where further training was needed. Focus group discussions and interviews with key stakeholders was also conducted to gain a deeper understanding of the challenges faced and the opportunities for capacity building.
  • Gap Analysis: The data collected from the training needs assessment was analysed to identify the gaps and challenges in the capacity-building initiatives.
  • Framework Development: Based on the findings of the training needs assessment and gap analysis, a capacity-building framework was developed that outlines the specific areas where training was needed, the target audience for each training module, and the training approach.
  • Training Module Development: Three training modules was developed to enhance the capacities of key stakeholders. The modules were generic in nature and sector-specific, focusing on Agriculture and Biodiversity and Sustainable Tourism. The modules developed were based on the findings of the training needs assessment and was designed to bridge the gaps identified during the gap analysis.
  • Training Delivery: The training modules was delivered to the key stakeholders identified during the stakeholder identification phase. The training was conducted through workshops, seminars, and online sessions.
  • Evaluation: The effectiveness of the training was evaluated using pre- and post-training assessments. The evaluation helps to identify the impact of the training on the knowledge, skills, and attitudes of the stakeholders and the effectiveness of the capacity-building framework. Based on the evaluation, the training modules and the framework were revised and updated as needed.

Conclusion

The project conducted a comprehensive training needs analysis to identify stakeholders and assess their current capacities and gaps to meet the objective of enhancing long-term biodiversity conservation in Sikkim's SECURE Himalayan Project landscapes. The analysis covered technical, managerial, and other aspects of organizational working, providing a clear view of the present scenario of biodiversity conservation in the state. Stakeholders were categorized into different groups based on their existing mandates and potential for mainstreaming biodiversity interventions. These groups included Core Line Departments, Core Enforcement Agencies, Non-Core Line Departments, Core Hand-holding Training Institutes/potential training implementers, and Community-Based organizations/Non-Governmental organizations. The Core Line Departments were further divided into senior and grassroot level officers based on their roles and responsibilities within the department.
